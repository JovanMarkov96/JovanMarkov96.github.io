A method for calibrating the momentum scale in a particle physics detector is described. The method relies on the determination of the masses of the final state particles in two-body decays of neutral particles, which can then be used to obtain corrections in the momentum scale. A modified version of the Armenteros-Podolanski plot and the {% raw %}$K_S^0 \to \pi^+ \pi^-${% endraw %} decay is used as a proof of principle. A precision at the {% raw %}$10^{-6}${% endraw %}–{% raw %}$10^{-8}${% endraw %} level is achieved in simplified simulations.
